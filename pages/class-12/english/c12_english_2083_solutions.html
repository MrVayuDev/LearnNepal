Section A: Comprehension

15 Marks
Passage Text (Scrollable)

The Magical Library

In a quiet corner of the bustling city, there stood an old, dusty library. While most people hurried past, its unassuming exterior hid an incredible secret. The librarian, an elderly woman named Ms. Abernathy, was not an ordinary librarian; she was the guardian of a magical library.

One sunny afternoon, Sarah, an eighth-grader with an insatiable love for books, stumbled upon the library. She had always been drawn to stories of magic and adventure, and something about the library's unassuming facade piqued her curiosity. As she stepped inside, a small bell above the door tinkled, announcing her arrival. Ms. Abernathy greeted her with a warm smile, sensing Sarah’s keen interest in books.

Sarah soon discovered the true enchantment of the library. Its shelves held volumes that could transport readers to other worlds. She found herself immersed in tales of knights and dragons, enchanted forests, and talking animals. These books had a way of making her feel like she was right there, part of the adventure.

As the weeks passed, Sarah spent hours at the library, delving into one captivating story after another. She uncovered a book about a boy who could fly, and as she read, she felt her feet lifting off the ground. She found herself soaring over the city, experiencing the thrill of flight. Another day, she read about a time-traveling watch, and suddenly, she was witnessing historical events unfold before her eyes.

One day, as Sarah was browsing the shelves, she noticed a book titled “The Lost Amulet of Althoria.” The cover featured a golden amulet with a shimmering blue gem. Ms. Abernathy explained that this was a unique book—it was unfinished. No one knew how the story ended because no reader had ever returned the book. Sarah decided to take on the challenge. She opened the book and was immediately drawn into a tale of a young adventurer, Alaric, on a quest to find the lost amulet. She felt herself journeying through dark forests, crossing treacherous rivers, and facing mythical creatures. The more she read, the deeper she became entangled in the story. It was as though she had become Alaric, feeling his determination and bravery.

As the days turned into weeks, Sarah's friends and family grew concerned. She had become so absorbed in the book that she neglected her usual activities. Ms. Abernathy gently reminded her to take breaks, but Sarah was determined to reach the end of the story.

Finally, one evening, as Sarah read the last page, she found herself standing in a clearing, facing an ornate door. Through the door, she saw a dazzling chamber with the lost amulet resting on a pedestal. With a deep breath, she opened the door and approached the amulet. As she touched it, a burst of blue light enveloped her, and she felt herself returning to the library.

When she opened her eyes, she was back among the dusty bookshelves, holding the book in her hands. Ms. Abernathy congratulated her on completing the unfinished story. Sarah had not only finished the tale but had also experienced an adventure unlike any other.

From that day on, Sarah continued to visit the magical library, understanding that the real magic was not just in the stories but in the way they made her feel and the adventures they allowed her to live.

1(A) Fill in the blanks with the correct highlighted words.
a. World leaders are now united in their ...... for peace.
b. People are impressed by her ........ manner and relaxed style of writing.
c. As I started to live on my own, I ........ myself in my career.
d. The stuff written about me helped to establish me as a/an ......... figure in public.
e. His desire for money is ........ .
Solution & Analysis
  1. World leaders are now united in their quest for peace.
  2. People are impressed by her unassuming manner and relaxed style of writing.
  3. As I started to live on my own, I immersed myself in my career.
  4. The stuff written about me helped to establish me as an ordinary figure in public.
  5. His desire for money is insatiable.

Section B: Literature

20 Marks
2(a) How would you react if you were in the young man's situation at the end of the story? (Neighbours)
Solution & Analysis

If I were in the young husband's situation at the end of "Neighbours", I would feel deeply moved, humbled, and grateful. Witnessing the overwhelming, genuine joy and support of the immigrant neighbours during the birth of his child would dissolve all my previous prejudices and cultural wariness. I would weep just as he did, realizing that human connection, empathy, and community transcend linguistic and cultural barriers, and I would fully embrace them as family.

2(b) How was Gouvernail different from Mrs. Baroda's expectation? (A Respectable Woman) OR Do you think Rakesh was a devoted son? Explain. (A Devoted Son)
Solution & Analysis

Option A (A Respectable Woman):
Mrs. Baroda expected Gouvernail to be a demanding, cynical, and highly intellectual city man who would disrupt their quiet plantation life. Instead, she found him to be quiet, lovable, unassuming, and extremely easygoing. He had no desire to impress anyone, did not speak much, and spent his time enjoying the peaceful outdoor air. His gentle, non-intrusive nature surprised Mrs. Baroda and made her feel comfortable yet secretly drawn to him.

Option B (A Devoted Son):
Initially, Rakesh was an exemplary devoted son who respected his father, Varma, by touching his feet and dedicating his success to him. However, when Varma grew old and sick, Rakesh's devotion turned into clinical, authoritarian supervision. He strictly restricted his father's food and forced medications upon him. While Rakesh acted out of professional medical duty to prolong his father's life, he ignored his father's emotional needs, rendering his devotion oppressive rather than compassionate.

2(c) What does the poet say about God's greatness? (Every Morning I Wake)
Solution & Analysis

In "Every Morning I Wake," Dylan Thomas depicts God's greatness through His infinite mercy, love, and protection of ordinary human beings. The poet portrays God as a benevolent guardian who looks down upon the residents of Milk Wood with compassion, despite their moral and physical flaws. God's greatness lies in His readiness to bless, forgive, and watch over frail and imperfect creatures day and night, offering them hope and solace.

2(d) Where was the author when he was late for lunch? (On Libraries) OR What is the goal of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights? (Human Rights and the Age of Inequality)
Solution & Analysis

Option A (On Libraries):
In "On Libraries," the author was inside a library (such as the British Museum Library), deeply absorbed in reading and researching books. Lost in the world of literature and knowledge, he completely lost track of time, which caused him to be late for his lunch appointment.

Option B (Human Rights and the Age of Inequality):
The primary goal of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) is to establish a global framework that guarantees the fundamental dignity, freedom, and equal rights of every human being. It aims to protect individuals from state tyranny and oppression. However, the essay notes that while it succeeded in promoting civil and political rights, it has struggled to address the massive economic inequality in the modern era.

2(e) Do you think that August Strindberg portrays despair in a very powerful and realistic way? Give a reason. (Facing Death)
Solution & Analysis

Yes, August Strindberg portrays despair powerfully and realistically through Monsieur Durand in "Facing Death." Durand is bankrupt, starved, and hated by his daughters. His despair is realistic because it stems from financial ruin and familial breakdown. His ultimate decision to commit suicide by burning down the house to secure insurance money for his ungrateful daughters shows the tragic, extreme limits a desperate person will go to.

3(a) What was the reaction of the community to the old man with enormous wings and what does it show about humanity and compassion? (A Very Old Man with Enormous Wings) OR What are Nock's ideas about marriage in society? (Marriage as a Social Institution)
Solution & Analysis

Option A (A Very Old Man with Enormous Wings):
In Gabriel García Márquez's story, the community reacts to the old man with wings with superficial curiosity, greed, and exploitation. Pelayo and Elisenda cage him in a chicken coop, and the townspeople treat him like a circus sideshow, throwing stones, poking him with hot branding irons, and demanding miracles. The local priest, Father Gonzaga, suspects him because he cannot speak Latin, and eventually, the crowd abandons him for a more sensational attraction (the spider woman).

This reaction shows that humanity often fears, dehumanizes, and exploits what it does not understand. True compassion is replaced by commercial and personal exploitation. People objectify the winged man for entertainment and miracles, demonstrating that human empathy can be transactional, shallow, and highly intolerant of differences or deviations from expected grandeur.

Option B (Marriage as a Social Institution):
In "Marriage as a Social Institution," Stephen L. Nock argues that marriage is a vital social institution rather than just a private relationship built on romance. It establishes legal, moral, and social norms that govern adult behavior, assign responsibilities, and provide stable environments for raising children. It acts as a contract that connects couples to the larger community.

I agree with Nock's ideas. While romantic love is crucial for personal fulfillment, the institutional framework of marriage offers necessary societal stability. It encourages long-term commitment, mutual economic security, and clear legal protections for parents and children. By viewing marriage as a social institution, society fosters stability, parental responsibility, and mutual accountability that individual emotions alone cannot sustain.

3(b) Explain the following lines of the poem, 'A Day' with reference to the context:
The hills untied their bonnets,
The bobolinks begun.
Then I said softly to myself,
That must have been the sun!
Solution & Analysis

Context: These lines are taken from Emily Dickinson's descriptive poem 'A Day', which captures the beauty and awe of a sunrise.

Explanation: Here, the speaker describes the magical transition of dawn using vivid personification and auditory imagery. "The hills untied their bonnets" describes the morning mist rising and clearing away from the hilltops, metaphorically compared to hills taking off their bonnets (hats) to greet the morning light. "The bobolinks begun" refers to the sudden morning songs of the bobolinks (birds). Witnessing this sudden awakening of nature—the light unveiling the hills and the birds singing—the speaker softly realizes that the sun has risen. These lines beautifully convey the quiet, majestic, and transformative power of the sun as it awakens the earth.

4 Study the following table and interpret the information given in the table in about 150 words. Make comparison where necessary.
Monthly Sales Data for XYZ Company (in thousands of dollars)
Month Product A Product B Product C Total Sales
January 50 40 30 120
February 60 35 25 120
March 70 45 35 150
April 65 50 40 155
May 75 55 45 175
June 80 60 50 190
Solution & Analysis

The table details the monthly sales (in thousands of dollars) of three products (A, B, and C) for XYZ Company from January to June. Overall, the total sales of the company demonstrate a strong, positive growth trend, rising from $120,000 in January and February to a peak of $190,000 in June.

Product A remained the highest contributor throughout the entire period. Its sales began at $50,000 in January, steadily grew to $70,000 in March, dipped slightly to $65,000 in April, and then climbed to its peak of $80,000 in June. Product B was the second-highest performer, starting at $40,000, hit a low of $35,000 in February, and thereafter rose consistently to finish at $60,000 in June. Product C, while generating the lowest sales, followed a similar trajectory, starting at $30,000, dipping to $25,000 in February, and steadily growing to end at $50,000 in June. In comparison, all three products recorded their highest sales in June, highlighting a highly successful second quarter for the company.

5 It is often said, 'Save nature to save the future'. Write a letter to the editor of a national newspaper about the effects of environmental pollution in about 180 words.
Solution & Analysis

Kathmandu, Nepal
May 23, 2026

To,
The Editor,
The Rising Nepal,
Kathmandu, Nepal.

Subject: The Alarming Effects of Environmental Pollution.

Sir,
I would like to utilize the columns of your esteemed newspaper to highlight the critical issue of environmental pollution in Nepal. The adage, 'Save nature to save the future', is a stark warning that we must heed immediately.

Due to rapid, unplanned urbanization and industrial activity, our air and water resources are heavily polluted. Kathmandu's air quality is frequently hazardous, causing severe respiratory issues. Our sacred rivers, like the Bagmati, have become dumping grounds for municipal and industrial waste. Additionally, deforestation and land degradation aggravate landslides and climate change.

If we do not protect our natural ecosystem now, we are compromising the health and survival of future generations. The government must strictly enforce environmental laws, promote clean public transportation, and manage solid waste efficiently. Public participation in green campaigns is also essential. Let us act together to save our environment.

Yours faithfully,
A Concerned Citizen

6 Write an essay on walking as a physical activity and its role in building physical, mental and social behavior of an individual in about 300 words.
Solution & Analysis

Walking: A Path to Holistic Well-being

Walking is one of the most fundamental, natural, and accessible physical activities. Requiring no expensive equipment or memberships, it offers a powerful way to enhance physical, mental, and social well-being.

Physically, regular walking is a cornerstone of good health. It strengthens the cardiovascular system, improves blood circulation, and controls blood pressure. A daily brisk walk of thirty minutes helps manage body weight, boosts metabolism, and reduces the risk of chronic illnesses such as type 2 diabetes and heart disease. Furthermore, it is a low-impact exercise that strengthens muscles and joints, making it highly suitable for individuals of all age groups.

Mentally, walking is highly therapeutic. It stimulates the release of endorphins, which are the body's natural mood lifters. This helps significantly reduce stress, anxiety, and depressive feelings. Walking in natural green environments, often called "green walking," clears the mind, increases concentration, and fosters creativity. It serves as a peaceful break from the digital screens and fast-paced routine of modern life, allowing for healthy reflection and stress relief.

Socially, walking acts as a bridge for connection. Walking in neighborhood parks or walking clubs encourages interactions and builds a sense of community. It helps reduce social isolation and loneliness by offering opportunities to converse with others. Group walking promotes team spirit and collective motivation. Moreover, replacing short vehicle trips with walks reflects responsible social behavior by reducing pollution and carbon emissions.

In conclusion, walking is much more than a physical movement. It is a holistic practice that keeps the body fit, calms the mind, and fosters social connections. Incorporating walking into our daily lives is a simple yet profound step toward building a healthier and more connected society.

Section D: Grammar

10 Marks
7 Do as instructed in the brackets and rewrite the sentences.
Solutions with Grammatical Context
  • a. Health care in Nepal is not as expensive as it is in the US. (Identify the adjective.)
    → Answer: expensive
  • b. My car needs ............ right now. (Choose the correct form: repair/repairing/to repair/repaired)
    → Answer: repairing
    Context: The verb 'need' followed by a gerund ('repairing') indicates passive necessity.
  • c. This is the house. We spent our childhood in the house. (Join using appropriate relative pronoun.)
    → Answer: This is the house where we spent our childhood. (or: This is the house in which we spent our childhood.)
  • d. Sumsher along with his friends ........... me well. (Choose: know/knows/have known/knowing)
    → Answer: knows
    Context: Subject-verb agreement with parenthetical phrases like 'along with' aligns with the singular subject 'Sumsher'.
  • e. Don't stare ......... me. I have no mistake. (Use appropriate preposition.)
    → Answer: at
  • f. You ....... not tell anyone about this. It's too confidential. (Choose modal: may/must/would/could)
    → Answer: must
  • g. The customer ............ the machine before he complained about the error. (Write correct form of 'purchase')
    → Answer: had purchased
    Context: Past perfect tense ('had purchased') is used for the action completed before another past event ('complained').
  • h. We are close friends. We have not seen each other's houses. (Join with 'despite'.)
    → Answer: Despite being close friends, we have not seen each other's houses.
  • i. Yangma said, "You are my source of inspiration in life." (Change into indirect speech.)
    → Answer: Yangma told me that I was his/her source of inspiration in life.
  • j. Loku was presenting his project for the first time. (Change into passive voice.)
    → Answer: His project was being presented by Loku for the first time.

Section E: Vocabulary & Sounds

5 Marks
8 Choose and copy the correct alternatives.
Solutions & Explanations
  • a. It's a beautiful present for me. What is the word class of the underlined word 'present'?
    → Answer: (i) noun
    Explanation: In this context, 'present' is preceded by an adjective and an article, representing a tangible gift (noun).
  • b. Which of the following words comes before 'choice' in the dictionary? (choose, chrome, chide, chord)
    → Answer: (iii) chide
    Explanation: Alphabetical ordering: ch-i-d-e comes before ch-o-i-c-e, whereas choose, chord, and chrome come after choice.
  • c. The babysitter ........... the girl while her parents were not at home. (looked after, looked at, looked over, looked up)
    → Answer: (i) looked after
    Explanation: 'Look after' is a phrasal verb meaning to take care of someone.
  • d. Which of the following words takes the prefix 'il-'? (balance, fertile, legal, mobile)
    → Answer: (iii) legal
    Explanation: 'Il-' combined with 'legal' forms 'illegal' (meaning not legal).
  • e. Which of the following words has the same initial sound as 'chaos'? (chance, chemical, circus, cinema)
    → Answer: (ii) chemical
    Explanation: Both 'chaos' and 'chemical' begin with the hard /k/ sound, unlike 'chance' (/tʃ/), 'circus' (/s/), and 'cinema' (/s/).
